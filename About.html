Jordan Haynes graduated from Mohave Accelerated Learning Center in May of 2010. Eager to serve his country, gain experience in Military Information Systems, Mr. Haynes joined the United States Army as a Multi-channel Transmission Systems Operator and Mainteriner. Enthusiastic to pursue opportunities in his military carreer, he volunteered for Basic Airborne training to become an Army paratrooper. After completing initial entry training, he was assigned to the 112th Signal Battalion in Fort Bragg, North Carolina.

During his military service, he deployed to East/Central Africa and Afghanistan earning multiple recognitions. In Mr. Haynes's roles as a Military Information Technician, he gained experience in physical security, network configuration and security, tactical radio systems, satellite systems configuration, and systems helpdesk.

After transitioning from the US Army, Mr. Haynes began studying at Oklahoma City Community College, majoring in A.S. Business. He graduated from OCCC with a 3.8 GPA, earning the president's honor role twice. Upon graduating, his A.S. he was accepted to Price College of Business at the University of Oklahoma. At Price College, Mr. Haynes majored in International Business and Management Information Systems with a minor in Spanish.Mr. Haynes was given an opportunity to study abroad for a semester in City University of Hong Kong where he majored in Marketing and took private lessons in Mandarin. He built on his experiences of operations and technology abroad with the teachings of accomplished academics in the United States and Hong Kong (SAR).

Mr. Haynes will graduate from Price College of Business in December with a cumulative 3.68 GPA and honor's recognitions. He Pursues careers in Systems Security, Business Analysis, Business Systems Analysis, or Systems Administration. Mr. Haynes hopes to soon begin work toward MIT/MBA degrees.
